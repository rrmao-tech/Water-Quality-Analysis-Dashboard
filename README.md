# 🌊 Watershed Water Quality Intelligence Dashboard  
### Multi-Factor Environmental Risk Analysis (Power BI Project)

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is an interactive **Power BI dashboard** designed to analyze watershed water quality and environmental risk patterns.

It integrates multiple environmental factors such as rainfall, land use, nutrient concentration, and ecological indicators to understand pollution drivers and ecosystem response.

The goal is to support **data-driven environmental monitoring and watershed management decisions**.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Water quality management is often limited by:

- Fragmented environmental datasets  
- Difficulty identifying key pollution sources  
- Lack of visibility into land-use impact  
- Limited understanding of ecosystem response to pollution  

---

## 🧠 Project Objectives

- Identify key drivers of water quality degradation  
- Analyze spatial differences in pollution levels  
- Evaluate impact of rainfall on turbidity and runoff  
- Assess ecological consequences of environmental pollution  

---

## 🗂 Data Sources

This project uses a structured environmental dataset including:

- Water quality metrics (Phosphorus, Dissolved Oxygen, Turbidity)
- Land use classification (Agriculture, Urban, Forest)
- Rainfall data
- Ecological indicators (Fish population health)

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies

- Power BI Desktop  
- Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)  
- DAX (Data Modeling & KPI Calculation)  
- Data Visualization & Dashboard Design  

---

## ⚙️ Data Processing Workflow

- Data cleaning and preprocessing using Power Query  
- Data modeling and relationship building in Power BI  
- KPI creation using DAX:
  - Water Quality Index (WQI)
  - Average Phosphorus
  - Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
  - Turbidity Index  
- Interactive filtering using slicers (Date, Station, Land Use, Metrics)

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

- KPI cards for key environmental indicators  
- Land-use impact comparison  
- Rainfall vs turbidity correlation analysis  
- Ecosystem impact tracking (fish population trends)  
- Interactive metric selector for multi-variable exploration  

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Agricultural land use is the primary contributor to nutrient pollution  
- Rainfall significantly increases turbidity due to runoff and sediment transport  
- Forested areas act as natural buffers stabilizing water quality  
- Pollution events are associated with declines in aquatic ecosystem health  

---

## ⚠️ Environmental Impact

- Nutrient pollution is strongly linked to agricultural activity  
- Urban stormwater contributes to long-term diffuse contamination  
- Rainfall accelerates pollutant transport into waterways  
- Ecosystem health is highly sensitive to water quality changes  

---

## 🧭 Recommendations

- Establish buffer zones around agricultural areas to reduce runoff  
- Improve urban stormwater management systems  
- Increase monitoring during rainfall events  
- Prioritize intervention in high-risk pollution regions
